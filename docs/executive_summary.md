# Executive Insights Summary

## Executive Summary

The initial synthetic distribution dataset suggests that relationship-oriented and workplace-adjacent lead sources generate stronger conversion and asset outcomes than broad paid acquisition sources. Employer Plan Rollover, Client Referral, and Existing Client Expansion leads show the strongest combination of conversion rate and asset generation.

Advisor performance is uneven, with a small group of advisors producing a disproportionate share of net new assets. This creates an opportunity for leadership to identify repeatable behaviors from high performers and use those patterns for coaching, staffing, and lead routing decisions.

The first-pass retention risk logic flags a high share of clients, especially in the High Value segment. This is useful as an early warning view, but the threshold should be calibrated before it is used as a final executive KPI.

## Recommended Leadership Actions

1. Prioritize investment in Client Referral, Employer Plan Rollover, and Existing Client Expansion lead programs.
2. Review Paid Search economics before scaling spend, since conversion and asset outcomes trail relationship-based sources.
3. Analyze Advisor 24's workflow, lead mix, and activity patterns to identify coaching practices that can be shared with peers.
4. Create a High Value client outreach queue using the retention risk mart, then calibrate the risk criteria with business partners.
5. Use the compensation alignment mart to identify advisors whose incentive payouts are not aligned with converted clients or net new assets.

## Assumptions and Limitations

- The dataset is synthetic and designed for portfolio demonstration.
- Risk thresholds are first-pass business rules and should be calibrated with stakeholder input.
- Compensation alignment is evaluated using simplified monthly incentive logic.
- Market movement is simulated and should be separated from controllable advisor-driven net flows.
